Labrador Retriever

Labrador Retrievers are known for their friendly and outgoing nature. They are affectionate, intelligent, and eager to please, making them excellent family companions. Their playful disposition and loyalty, combined with their gentle demeanor, make them wonderful with children and adaptable to various living situations.

  • Life Span: 10-14 years
  • Weight: 55-80 lbs
  • Height: 21.5-24.5 inches
  • Main Diseases: Hip and Elbow Dysplasia, Obesity

    • German Shepherd

    German Shepherds are known for their exceptional intelligence, loyalty, and versatility. They are often employed as police and service dogs due to their keen sense of duty and trainability. Protective and devoted, they form strong bonds with their families. Their courage and confidence make them excellent guardians and companions.

  • Life Span: 9-13 years
  • Weight: 50-90 lbs
  • Height: 22-26 inches
  • Main Diseases: Hip and Elbow Dysplasia, Gastric Issues

    • Golden Retriever

    Golden Retrievers are known for their friendly and gentle character. They're affectionate, intelligent, and loyal, making them wonderful family pets and therapy dogs. Their love for people, especially children, along with their eagerness to please, contributes to their reputation as one of the most beloved and trustworthy breeds.

  • Life Span: 10-12 years
  • Weight: 55-75 lbs
  • Height: 21.5-24 inches
  • Main Diseases: Hip Dysplasia, Cancer

    • French Bulldog

    The French Bulldog is known for its affectionate and playful nature. Despite its small size, it's a sturdy and sociable companion. These dogs are loyal, intelligent, and well-mannered, making them great family pets. Their charming personality and adaptability to various living environments have earned them a special place in many hearts.

  • Life Span: 10-12 years
  • Weight: 16-28 lbs
  • Height: 11-12 inches
  • Main Diseases: Brachycephalic Syndrome, Joint Issues

    • Bulldog

    Bulldogs are known for their gentle and affectionate nature. Despite their intimidating appearance, they are friendly, loyal, and good with children. They have a calm demeanor, making them great companions for indoor living. Bulldogs are also determined and courageous, traits stemming from their historical roles in bull-baiting, which they've evolved from into beloved family pets.

  • Life Span: 8-10 years
  • Weight: 40-50 lbs
  • Height: 14-15 inches
  • Main Diseases: Respiratory Problems, Skin Infections

    • Poodle

    Poodles are renowned for their intelligence and elegance. With a playful and alert demeanor, they easily adapt to various environments. Their hypoallergenic coat, in various sizes (Standard, Miniature, Toy), requires regular grooming. Poodles are not only stylish companions but also excel in activities like obedience, agility, and assistance work due to their sharp intellect.

  • Life Span: 10-18 years
  • Weight: 45-70 lbs
  • Height: Standard: 15 inches or more, Miniature: 10-15 inches
  • Main Diseases: Hip Dysplasia, Eye Conditions

    • Beagle

    Beagles are known for their friendly and curious nature. They're playful, energetic, and have a keen sense of smell. With a charming disposition and a love for social interaction, Beagles often make excellent family pets. However, their strong scent instincts require proper training to ensure they stay focused and well-behaved.

  • Life Span: 12-15 years
  • Weight: 20-30 lbs
  • Height: 13-15 inches
  • Main Diseases: Epilepsy, Hip Dysplasia

    • Rottweiler

    The Rottweiler is a loyal and confident breed, often characterized by its protective nature. They are intelligent, trainable, and can be excellent family dogs with proper socialization. Their strong instincts make them natural guard dogs, while their affectionate side with their loved ones adds to their unique charm.

  • Life Span: 8-10 years
  • Weight: 80-135 lbs
  • Height: 24-27 inches
  • Main Diseases: Hip and Elbow Dysplasia, Heart Issues

    • Yorkshire Terrier

    The Yorkshire Terrier, or Yorkie, is a small yet confident and spirited breed. Despite their size, they possess a big personality. They're affectionate, energetic, and often have a feisty nature. Yorkies are known for their loyalty to their owners and their keen intelligence, making them delightful companions.

  • Life Span: 11-15 years
  • Weight: Up to 7 lbs
  • Height: 7-8 inches
  • Main Diseases: Dental Issues, Tracheal Collapse

    • Boxer

    The Boxer is known for its boundless energy and playful nature. It's a loyal and affectionate breed, often forming strong bonds with its family. Intelligent and eager to please, the Boxer is protective and great with kids. Its exuberance and athleticism make it a wonderful companion for active households.

  • Life Span: 10-12 years
  • Weight: 50-80 lbs
  • Height: 21.5-25 inches
  • Main Diseases: Heart Conditions, Cancer
